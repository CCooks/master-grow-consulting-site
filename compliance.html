Legal & Compliance

Growing cannabis is a privilege regulated by state and local laws. This page summarises key Nevada home grow requirements. Always consult official statutes for full details.

Nevada Home Grow Rules

  • Eligibility: You must be 21 years or older to cultivate cannabis at home.
  • Distance: Home cultivation is allowed only if you live more than 25 miles from the nearest licensed dispensary.
  • Plant limits: Up to 6 plants per adult (max 12 plants per household), including seedlings. Plants must be grown for personal use and not sold.
  • Secure enclosure: All plants must be grown in a locked, enclosed area that is not visible to the public.
  • Property rights: You must own the property or have written permission from the owner. Homeowners associations may set additional restrictions.
  • Compliance with city/county: Local governments may impose additional zoning or safety requirements. Always check municipal regulations.

Licensed Cultivation Compliance

For those working in or operating licensed cultivation facilities, strict rules apply. These include:

  • Obtaining the appropriate licence (cultivation, production, distribution, testing, etc.) and meeting financial and background requirements.
  • Submitting detailed facility plans and equipment lists to the Nevada Cannabis Compliance Board before opening.
  • Using Metrc seed‑to‑sale tracking with RFID tags and maintaining accurate records.
  • Adhering to pesticide disclosure and packaging regulations.
  • Maintaining strict security, sanitation and environmental controls to safeguard product and personnel.

How We Operate

Master Grow Consulting provides advisory and educational services. We do not sell, transport or handle cannabis products. Our team respects state and federal laws and guides clients to remain compliant. Our technicians carry insurance and follow safety protocols for in‑home visits.
